Frequently Asked Questions

Who maintains CoreBR?

CoreBR is maintained by the Applied Software Engineering Research Group, from Federal University of Minas Gerais, Brazil. However, the project results are not endorsed by UFMG or any Brazilian research agency. The prject also does not have any connection with Brazilian Qualis Ranking.

How CoreBR works?

Papers are collected, using scripts, from DBLP. We only collect full papers, accepted in the main research track of the considered conferences.

What is your definition of a full paper?

It is a paper presented in the main track of a conference. We also apply the following minimum page size thresholds:

  • Software Engineering, Programming Languages, Information Systems, Databases, Web & Information Retrieval: 10 pages
  • Computer Networks, Distributed Systems, Human-Computer Interaction: 8 pages
Do you plan to cover short papers (including demos, tool papers, early research papers, papers in industry track etc)?

No, the focus is on full papers, describing mature and carefully evaluated work. Papers tracked by CoreBR can be considered as journal-quality papers, although published in conferences.

Do you plan to cover journals?

Yes, but only after covering conferences in all CS research areas.

Why my paper is not listed?

Please, check if it is a full paper, published in the main track of the conferences tracked by CoreBR, in the last five years (2013-2017). If this the case, please open an issue on GitHub, informing your name, department, and info about the paper (title, conference year).

Which conferences are tracked?

We do not intend to cover all CS conferences. The number we have in mind is 15 conferences per research area. For each area, it is mandatory to cover the top conferences, as classified by CSRankings.

Why conference [Conf] is not covered?

We accept suggestions to track other conferences. However, our main interest is on high-quality conferences, with good metrics (submitted > 100 papers; acceptance < 30%; h5-index > 20) and well-known sponsors (ACM SIGs, IEEE CS, etc). If you think this is the case of [Conf], please open an issue on GitHub, informing the statistics about this conference in 2017 (see the required statistics in the Stats tab).

How is the departments score computed?
Using this formula: score = A + (0.66 * B) + (0.33 * C), where A is the number of papers in top conferences (according to CSRankings); B is the number of papers in "below-the-fold" conferences (the ones only listed after clicking in the name of a research area in the CSRankings main menu); and C is the number of papers in the remaining conferences tracked by CoreBR. It is important to mention that not all areas have "below-the-fold" conferences, according to the CSRankings classification.

How can I help so that CoreBR covers an research area X?

First, enter in contact providing basic metrics about the core conferences in X, in our opinion (see the required metrics in the Stats tab).

What happens when an author moves from institution X to Y?

CoreBR assigns all papers from an author to its current institution. Therefore, after moving to Y, his papers are listed as belonging to Y, even the ones produced when he was at X. The main reason is that it is not trivial to automatically keep track of the past institutions of each author in our database.
