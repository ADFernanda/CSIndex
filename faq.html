Frequently Asked Questions

Who maintain CoreBR

CoreBR is maintained by the Applied Software Engineering Research Group, from Federal University of Minas Gerais, Brazil. The site is not endorsed by UFMG or any Brazilian research agency.

How CoreBR works?

Papers are collected, using scripts, from DBLP. We only collect full papers, accepted in the main research track of the considered conferences.

How are the results presented?

The data collected by CoreBR is presented using Google spreadsheets, in four sheets:

  • Summary: number of full research papers with at least one Brazilian author published in the conferences tracked by CoreBR during the period 2013-2017
  • Profs: Brazilian researchers tracked by CoreBR
  • Papers: data about the papers collected by CoreBR
  • Confs: sponsors and other statistics about the conferences considered by CoreBR

What conferences are tracked?

First, it is important to mention that there is no predefined limit on the number of conferences in each research area. But we do not intend to cover a large number of conferences. The number we have in mind is at most 15 conferences per research area. For each area, it is mandatory to cover the top conferences and the below-the-fold conferences, as considered by CSRankings.

Why my paper is not listed?

Please, first check if it is a full paper, published in the conferences tracked by CoreBR (listed in the Confs sheet), in the last five years (2013-2017). If this is the case, also check if your name is listed in the Profs sheet (which is a list of the Brazilian researchers tracked by CoreBR). If not, please open an issue at GitHub, informing your name and PID at DBLP.

Why conference [Conf] is not covered?

We accept suggestions to track other conferences. However, as the name of the project suggests, our main interest is on high-quality conferences, with good metrics (number of submitted/accepted papers, h6 index, etc) and also well-known sponsors (ACM SIGs, IEEE CS, etc). If you think this is the case of [Conf], please open an issue at GitHub, mentioning the 2017 statistics abut this conference (see the required statistics in the Confs sheet, in the spreadsheet with the results provided by CoreBR).

How can I help so that CoreBR covers an research area X?

First, enter in contact providing basic statistics about the core conferences in X, in our opinion (see the required statistics in the Confs sheet, in the spreadsheet with the results provided by CoreBR). If we approve your list of conferences, you will also need to provide a list of Brazilian researchers in area X, including their DBLP PIDs (see Authors sheet).

What is your definition of research area?

A research area must have a Special Interest Group (Comissao Especial) at SBC.

Do you plan to cover journals?

Yes, but only after covering conferences in a representative number of CS research areas.

Do you plan to track papers from other countries?

Yes, but not in the near future.
